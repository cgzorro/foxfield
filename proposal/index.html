Stock it right · Orderflow

The clipboard, finally counting for you.

Ordering and receiving built for the dock, not the back office. The order is the expected list; receiving checks the truck against it. The order carries a QR — scan it off the cart with any phone and that expected list opens, already filled in. Most lines come in as ordered — you confirm them in one tap. You only put a finger on what came up short. The bar is simple: it has to be faster than paper, or it doesn't belong on your floor.

  • Touch only the exceptions — paper makes you write every line; this doesn't
  • Scan the cart's QR — any phone opens the order's expected list, no app to install
  • Variance caught at the dock, not three weeks later on the bench
  • A grower scorecard builds itself — order around the farms that short you
  • Your allocation rules when a truck runs short — you set them, you approve every split

Two gaps get caught in one scan: what the grower shipped vs. what you ordered (the QR), and what actually arrived vs. their invoice (your taps). The farm QR needs the grower on the shared sheet — but receiving still works without them, pre-filling from your order, and the QR switches on grower by grower as they join. The floor apps are live today, and scan-to-receive runs in the demo right now — hardening it for big orders and a dead-signal dock is the off-season build.

Why it holds together

One spine, not three apps.

The quiet part that makes this defensible: Orderflow's receiving generates the living catalog that Wayfinder and Plant Consultant run on. You digitize what comes off the truck once, and every tool downstream stays accurate without anyone re-typing a thing. Start with one tool; the same spine grows into all three.
